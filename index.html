Research
Interests

Dr. Ranjit Podderâ€™s research spans a broad range of topics within the field of education, with a particular focus on English Language Teaching (ELT), curriculum development, assessment practices, and teacher training. His key research interests include:

  • English Language Teaching (ELT): Investigating the challenges of teaching English at secondary levels in Bangladesh, including communicative language teaching (CLT) and improving aural-oral skills assessments.
  • Assessment Practices: Studying alternative and informal assessment techniques, including listening and speaking skills, particularly in secondary schools. His research also examines the barriers and enablers for teachers in assessing English language skills.
  • Curriculum and Learning Theories: Exploring the implementation of the 2012 secondary curriculum in Bangladesh, addressing expectations and challenges faced by educators.
  • Teacher Development and Mentoring: Focusing on professional development strategies for teacher educators through mentoring and reflective practices.
  • Educational Technology: Examining the role of digital literacy and the use of platforms like Facebook to enhance learners' English writing skills.

Dr. Podder has published extensively in both national and international journals, with notable works on educational change in Bangladesh and the use of innovative teaching strategies in secondary education. He also serves as the editor of the Bangladesh Teacher Education Journal (BTEJ), contributing to the discourse on teacher education and pedagogical practices.
